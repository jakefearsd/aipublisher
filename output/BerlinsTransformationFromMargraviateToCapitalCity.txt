!! History
BerlinsTransformationFromMargraviateToCapitalCity began with Berlin's founding around 1163 by Albert the Bear, who established it as part of the larger territory of Brandenburg. In 1415, Brandenburg became a Margraviate under the House of Hohenzollern. By the early 8th century, Brandenburg-Prussia was elevated to the Kingdom of Prussia with Berlin at its heart.

During this time, Berlin played crucial roles in historical events such as the Protestant Reformation and the Thirty Years' War. The city's cultural prominence grew significantly under Frederick II (Frederick the Great), who ruled from 1740 to 1786 and transformed Berlin into a major center of Enlightenment thought.

!! Cultural Development during the Enlightenment
The reign of Frederick II marked a golden age for Berlin, characterized by rapid cultural development. Artists like Johann Gottfried Herder and philosophers such as Immanuel Kant flourished in this intellectual environment. Buildings designed by Karl Friedrich Schinkel further enhanced Berlin's status as an architectural marvel.
