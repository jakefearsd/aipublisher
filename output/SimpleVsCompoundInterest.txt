This page provides a comparison between simple interest and compound interest, explaining how each is calculated and when it might be more advantageous to use one over the other in financial contexts.

!! Introduction to Simple and Compound Interest
Simple interest and compound interest are two methods of calculating interest on borrowed or invested money. The choice between them can significantly impact your financial outcomes, especially over longer periods.
The primary difference lies in how each type accumulates over time: simple interest is calculated solely on the principal amount, whereas compound interest includes both the principal and previously accumulated interest, leading to exponential growth.

!! Calculating Simple Interest
Simple interest is computed using a straightforward formula: I = P * r * t. Here,
P represents the principal amount (the initial sum of money),
r stands for the rate of interest per period, and
t denotes the time in years or fractions thereof during which the interest accrues.
This method is typically used for short-term loans or single-period investments where there's no need to account for growth on previously earned interest.

!! Understanding Compound Interest
Compound interest can be compounded at various intervals such as annually, semi-annually, quarterly, monthly, and even continuously. Continuous compounding utilizes the mathematical constant e (approximately 2.71828) to calculate interest growth over time without interruption.
The formula for compound interest is A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt), where A is the amount of money accumulated after n years, including interest; P is the principal amount; r is the annual interest rate (decimal); n is the number of times that interest is compounded per year; and t is the time in years.

!! Comparing Simple vs Compound Interest
Both methods serve distinct purposes within finance, making them applicable under different circumstances:

* __Simple Interest__
- Best for short-term financial products such as car loans or personal lines of credit.
- Easy to compute and understand.

* __Compound Interest__
- Suitable for long-term investments like savings accounts, bonds, and retirement funds.
- Offers higher returns due to exponential growth over time through the reinvestment of accrued interest.

!! Applications in Finance
Simple interest is commonly applied in scenarios where interest rates do not fluctuate significantly or where there's no intention to reinvest earned interest. Conversely, compound interest finds its utility in financial products that benefit from reinvestment and longer holding periods for maximizing returns.

|| Application || Simple Interest || Compound Interest ||
| Short-term loans | Yes | No |
| Savings accounts | Rarely | Commonly |
| Bonds | Limited | Widespread |
